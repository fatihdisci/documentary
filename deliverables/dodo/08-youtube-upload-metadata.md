## Recommended public title

The Dodo: How an Island Bird Vanished

## Description

The dodo was not a foolish cartoon. It was a large, flightless pigeon found nowhere but Mauritius—until people and introduced animals transformed its island home.

This documentary follows the evidence: what the dodo was, why its isolated world made it vulnerable, and what its disappearance still tells us about island conservation.

The last widely accepted sighting was in 1662. The exact date of extinction is uncertain, but the dodo disappeared during the seventeenth century, soon after colonisation began.

Sources and further reading:
Roberts, D. L. & Solow, A. R. (2003), “When did the dodo become extinct?”, Nature. https://doi.org/10.1038/426245a
Natural History Museum, London: https://www.nhm.ac.uk/discover/news/2023/march/extinctions-island-dodo-pushing-plants-towards-extinction.html
