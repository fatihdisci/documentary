## YouTube description

The thylacine looked like a striped dog, but it was something far older and stranger: the last modern member of a unique family of meat-eating marsupials.

This documentary follows the evidence from its ancient range across Australia and New Guinea to its final refuge in Tasmania. It examines the bounty system, the other pressures that may have deepened the decline, and the new museum research that corrected the story of the last known animal.

The final thylacine died at Hobart Zoo on 7 September 1936. Protection had begun only 59 days earlier.
