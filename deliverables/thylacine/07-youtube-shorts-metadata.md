Protected 59 Days Too Late

### Alternative titles

- The Last Thylacine Wasn’t Benjamin
- The Footage Came Near the End

### Description

The last known thylacine died at Hobart Zoo on 7 September 1936.

Legal protection had begun only 59 days earlier. New museum research also corrected a long-held story: the last animal was an older female caught in 1936, not the famous animal once called Benjamin.

Later sightings never produced clear proof. The film, skins, bones, and museum records are what remain.
