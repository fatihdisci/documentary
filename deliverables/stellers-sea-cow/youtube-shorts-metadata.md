### Description

Steller's sea cows ate kelp in the cold shallows of the northern Pacific.

Scientists are still exploring what a giant grazer like this may have meant for those forests. By feeding near the surface, the animals may have changed how light reached the kelp below.

We cannot rebuild every detail. But this was more than a huge animal drifting through the water. It was part of a living coastal system.

The final population lived in a small area and could recover slowly. Repeated hunting was enough to push this giant of the kelp forest over the edge. No rescue plan existed. No one was counting the last survivors.

One of the northern Pacific's largest animals vanished within a single human lifetime.
