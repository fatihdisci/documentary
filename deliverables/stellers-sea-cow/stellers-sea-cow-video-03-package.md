## Research summary

Steller's sea cow was a huge plant-eating marine mammal of the cold North Pacific. Historical observations place the final known population around Bering and Medny Islands in the Commander Islands. Georg Wilhelm Steller encountered it while stranded on Bering Island in 1741; he was the only scientist known to observe it alive. The animal was adapted to shallow coastal kelp habitat and had a dark, thick, bark-like skin. Adults near Bering Island were about 7.5–8 metres long, although exact maximum size remains uncertain.

Hunting for meat, fat, and hides, combined with the animal's restricted range and slow population recovery, drove the final population down quickly. The accepted extinction date is 1768, about 27 years after the first scientific observation. The exact wider ecological role of the species is still being investigated, so the narration frames kelp-forest effects as a scientific possibility rather than a fact.
