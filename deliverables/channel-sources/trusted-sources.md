# Trusted Sources

## Primary sources
- IUCN Red List of Threatened Species
- BirdLife International
- Catalogue of Life
- World Register of Marine Species
- Integrated Taxonomic Information System
- GBIF

## Museums and institutions
- Smithsonian Institution
- Natural History Museum, London
- American Museum of Natural History
- Australian Museum
- Museum of New Zealand Te Papa Tongarewa
- Official university museums and zoological collections

## Government and conservation
- New Zealand Department of Conservation
- Australian Government environment agencies
- U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service
- National Park Service
- Official biodiversity and conservation authorities

## Academic sources
- Peer-reviewed zoology and conservation papers
- Conservation Biology
- Biological Conservation
- Journal of Biogeography
- Science
- Nature
- PLOS ONE
- DOI-linked original research

## Rules
1. Confirm accepted scientific name and taxonomic status.
2. Distinguish Extinct, Extinct in the Wild, Possibly Extinct and Functionally Extinct.
3. Distinguish last confirmed sighting from official declaration date.
4. Do not present one extinction cause as the sole cause without evidence.
5. Use cautious wording where dates or causes are disputed.
6. Prefer primary and official sources over blogs and listicles.
7. Do not cite AI summaries as sources.
